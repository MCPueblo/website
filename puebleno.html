Puebleño

Purchasing the Puebleño role is how MCPueblo can sustain itself and also allow for bonus features that we would never have without Puebleños (e.g. the whitelist bot, the head shop, the /disguise command, etc.) As seen below, Puebleños have many different perks that you can purchase with points, including /maptool, /disguise, /nick, and /particles.
You receive 100 points per each dollar you contribute with Puebleño, and you can pay whatever you want for the role, with a minimum of $5 due to processing fees by PayPal. Note that we process our payments with PayPal, but you do not need to have an account, only a credit card.

To get Puebleño and support MCPueblo, go to store.mcpueblo.net!

Perks

To purchase perks with points, you must run the command /perks when in-game. You can still view the perks even without the Puebleño role.

Perk Points (100 = $1) Description
/setcolor 300 Make yourself unique by setting a chat color
/disguise (passive mobs) 400 Transform into any passive mob
/disguise (hostile mobs) 200 Transform into any hostile mob (Must purchase passive disguises first)
/particles 400 Have a wide variety of particle effects follow you
/maptool 500 Place images in-game
/hat 100 Wear any block as an item
/nick (color/formatting only) 200 Change your username's color and formatting
/nick (full customization) 400 Change your nickname to anything that follows the rules (Must purchase colored names first)
Total 2500

/maptool

You can create maps with images using the above command. Instructions on how to use the command correctly are:

  • /tomap <URL> - By default this will create a 1:1 pixel accurate map. Make sure the URL is a direct link to the image e.g. https://mcpueblo.net/assets/images/logo.png and not a link to a page like https://imgur.com/gallery/lhOLV
  • /tomap <URL> resize - This will resize the image to fit in a single map.
  • /tomap <URL> resize-stretched [w] [h] - This will stretch the image to the very edges of the map. You may also specific a width and height in blocks for the image to stretch to those dimensions.
  • /tomap <URL> resize-covered [w] [h] - This will resize the image cover the whole map area without stretching. Specify a width and height in blocks for the image to cover without stretching.
  • /maps - Opens a GUI to manage all your maps.

Here are a few rules in order to prevent abuse, not following these will result in a Rule #1 violation:

  1. Each player may have a maximum of 49 maps in total, equivalent to a single 7x7 image.
  2. You may not sell map images to anyone, not even in private.
  3. No inappropiate or offensive content on the maps.

/nick

Set a nickname to change your name or color. Remember that the first nickname perk only supports setting your colors and formattings, and not change the actual name itself, for that you need to buy both perks. Here are a few examples, with JuanDelPueblo as the base username:

  • /nick &6&lJuanDelPueblo = JuanDelPueblo - Sets the color and formatting of your name. Use the color codes from this guide with & as the prefix. Color goes before formatting as seen in this example.
  • /nick &#b366ffJuan&#00bd58Del&#b366ffPueblo = JuanDelPueblo - A new 1.16 feature, you can now set ANY color possible with hex codes! Google "hex color picker" to find a color you want, then insert in the nickname as &#FFFFFF, the 6 characters being the color.
  • /nick &aJuannie = Juannie - If you have both /nick perks, you can set any nickname you'll as long as you follow the below guidelines.

You must also follow these rules for nicknames:

  1. Do not imimate other members, especially not staff members.
  2. No inappropiate or offensive names.
  3. No hard-to-read names (don't use black or very dark colors, special symbols, strikethrough, etc.)

/particles

There are hundreds of possible particle effects and shapes, and you can combine multiple of them.
You can set arrow effects, effects that follow you, effects on block break/place, rotating cube, damage effects, attack effects, and more all using a simple GUI!

/disguise

The full list of disguises depends whenever you have the first perk or both perks, but they can be viewed in-game. Remember to not abuse disguises, or they will be restricted or even removed altogether.

  • /disguise <mob> - Disguise as mob
  • /disguiseviewself - Toggle between viewing your own disguise or not (recommended off for large mobs)
  • /undisguise - Removes your disguise

/hat

You can wear any blocks in your head, including banners.
